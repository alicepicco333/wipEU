How do the levels of representation of women in different decision-making positions correlate within the European Union?


Is there a discernible pattern or trend in the relationships between women occupying various roles, and how have these correlations evolved over time?

About

About the Project

wipEU is an open data project regarding the analysis of factors that might influence the presence of women in position of Power & Decision Making in the EU.

We have decided to approach this topic by analyzing a few fundamental areas:


Public Administration
Business & Finance
Politics
Environment & Climate Change
Education, Science & Research
Law & Institutions

In addition to addressing the primary research question, this project involves comprehensive examinations of various facets related to open data, including considerations of quality, legality, ethics, and technical aspects. Diverse visualizations are available to facilitate effective communication and enhance comprehension of the findings. Users have the option to freely download a thoroughly detailed documentation for reference.

Results


In 2023:

Women members of the highest decision-making body in European Financial Institutions 20%
Women members of the highest decision-making body in European agencies for Public Administration 37%
Women members of the highest decision-making body in European Research Funding organizations 43.1%
Women members of European Court for Justice 22.2%
Women presidents or chairs of European Financial Institutions 66.7%
Women Leading Major Political Parties 26.8%

Visualizations

Choropleth Maps

Explore the datasets through choropleth maps, offering a visual representation of geographic-related data for a comprehensive understanding.

*When value is undefined, the country is either geographically part of Europe but not of the EU, or the data are not available for the selected country. However, data regarding the UK are still present.






Line Charts

The line charts serve as dynamic visualizations of the gender distribution within European institutions across the years. With time represented along the X-axis and the number of individuals on the Y-axis, distinct lines trace the evolving counts of men and women.

These charts offer a clear and concise depiction of how gender composition has changed over time, facilitating a nuanced understanding of trends and potential disparities.

Sustainability of the Project

Sustainability

The Gender Statistics Database, from where we retrieved the data, contains gender statistics from all over the European Union (EU) and beyond, at the EU, Member State and European level.
It is aimed at providing statistical evidence which can be used to support and complement the European Commission’s (EC) Strategy on Gender Equality and support the Member States to monitor their progress.

However, wipEU is the final project developed for the "Open Access and Digital Ethics" course (a.a. 2023/2024) within the Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge Masters Degree (University of Bologna), and, as such is not actively maintained and will not be updated in the future.
